CONQUEST MOTHER

by Ar3ne
Atop her throne of briars and bone,
The queen reigns, her conquest hushed.
My hand, torn by her sweet lament,
Bears a nail driven through stone and silken rust.

A giant slumbers in the withered plains,
Her grave a sprawl of splintered cross and shale.
From her spine, a lineage of grief unfurls,
Her cloak, a shroud, exacts the toll of farewell.

Mothers to our toppled groves, they stand—
Regret, a quickening pang, claws at the end.
In anguish’s bloom, we cling to fallen boughs,
Our roots entwined with hers, unyielding, condemned.
